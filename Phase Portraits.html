Phase Portraits

Phase portraits can be used to visualize solutions for a system of linear equations, in this case of the form \[\vec{X}'=A\vec{X}\] The axes of phase space are the values of the functions \(x\) and \(y\) which both depend on an independent variable \(t\). In this case, \(\vec{X}=\begin{bmatrix} x(t)\\y(t)\end{bmatrix}\). Lines in drawn in the phase space represent how \(x\) and \(y\) evolve over time depending on the initial conditions.

In the phase space, the origin is a fixed point, meaning any solution with initial conditions at the origin will stay at the origin forever. The behavior of all other solution curves depend on the eigenvalues of the solution. For example, the node could be stable, meaning all solutions converge at the origin, or the node could be unstable, meaning all solutions move away from the origin.
